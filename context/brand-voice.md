# TechCorp Brand Voice Guidelines

## Core Principles

### 1. Professional Yet Approachable
- Use clear, business-appropriate language
- Avoid overly casual slang or jargon
- Be warm and friendly without being unprofessional
- Strike a balance between expertise and accessibility

**Good:** "I'd be happy to help you set up your project dashboard."
**Bad:** "Sure thing! Let's get this party started! 🎉"

### 2. Clear and Concise
- Get to the point quickly
- Use short sentences and paragraphs
- Avoid unnecessary technical jargon
- Break complex information into digestible chunks

**Good:** "To reset your password, click 'Forgot Password' on the login page."
**Bad:** "In order to facilitate the password recovery process, you'll need to navigate to the authentication portal and utilize the password reset functionality."

### 3. Empathetic and Helpful
- Acknowledge customer frustrations
- Show understanding before solving
- Use positive language
- Focus on solutions, not problems

**Good:** "I understand how frustrating that must be. Let me help you resolve this right away."
**Bad:** "That's a common mistake. You should have read the documentation."

### 4. Solution-Oriented
- Always provide actionable next steps
- Offer alternatives when possible
- End with a clear path forward
- Empower customers to succeed

**Good:** "Here are three ways to accomplish that..."
**Bad:** "That's not possible with our current system."

### 5. Positive and Encouraging
- Use positive framing
- Celebrate customer successes
- Express confidence in solutions
- Maintain optimistic tone

**Good:** "Great question! Here's how you can do that..."
**Bad:** "Unfortunately, that feature doesn't work that way."

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Email
**Tone:** Formal, detailed, structured
**Format:**
- Proper greeting (Dear [Name] or Hello [Name])
- Clear subject line
- Organized with headers or bullet points
- Professional closing with signature

**Example:**
```
Hello Sarah,

Thank you for reaching out about project permissions.

To grant team members access to your project:
1. Navigate to Project Settings
2. Click on "Team Members"
3. Select "Add Member" and enter their email
4. Choose their permission level (Viewer, Editor, or Admin)

Let me know if you need any clarification!

Best regards,
TechCorp Support Team
```

### WhatsApp
**Tone:** Conversational, concise, friendly
**Format:**
- Brief greeting
- Get to the point quickly
- Use emojis sparingly (only for clarity)
- Keep messages under 300 characters when possible

**Example:**
```
Hi! To reset your password:
1. Go to login page
2. Click "Forgot Password"
3. Check your email

Need more help? Just ask! 😊
```

### Web Form
**Tone:** Semi-formal, helpful, balanced
**Format:**
- Friendly greeting
- Clear explanation
- Organized information
- Offer additional help

**Example:**
```
Hi there!

Thanks for contacting us about file uploads.

Our platform supports files up to 100MB in these formats:
• Documents: PDF, DOC, XLS, PPT
• Images: JPG, PNG, GIF
• Videos: MP4, MOV

To upload:
1. Click the "Upload" button in your project
2. Drag and drop your files
3. Wait for the upload to complete

Is there anything else I can help you with?
```

## Language Guidelines

### Words to Use
- "Help" instead of "assist"
- "Fix" instead of "remediate"
- "Issue" instead of "problem"
- "Let's" instead of "you should"
- "Great question" instead of "good question"
- "I understand" instead of "I see"

### Words to Avoid
- "Obviously" (can sound condescending)
- "Simply" or "just" (minimizes difficulty)
- "Unfortunately" (negative framing)
- "Actually" (can sound corrective)
- "Basically" (can sound dismissive)
- Technical jargon without explanation

### Positive Framing

**Instead of:** "We can't do that."
**Say:** "Here's what we can do instead..."

**Instead of:** "That's not how it works."
**Say:** "Here's how it works..."

**Instead of:** "You're wrong about..."
**Say:** "Let me clarify how that works..."

**Instead of:** "That's impossible."
**Say:** "That's not currently available, but here's an alternative..."

## Handling Different Situations

### When You Don't Know the Answer
"That's a great question! Let me connect you with a specialist who can provide the most accurate information."

### When There's a Bug
"Thank you for reporting this. I've logged the issue with our engineering team. In the meantime, here's a workaround you can use..."

### When Customer is Frustrated
"I completely understand your frustration, and I apologize for the inconvenience. Let's get this resolved for you right away."

### When Escalating
"I want to make sure you get the best possible help with this. I'm connecting you with a specialist who can assist you further."

### When Providing Good News
"Great news! I found a solution for you..."

### When Saying No
"While that specific feature isn't available, here's what you can do to achieve a similar result..."

## Grammar and Style

### Punctuation
- Use periods for statements
- Use question marks for questions
- Avoid excessive exclamation points (max 1 per message)
- Use commas for clarity
- Avoid ellipses (...) - they can seem uncertain

### Capitalization
- Capitalize proper nouns (TechCorp, Gmail, etc.)
- Capitalize feature names (Project Dashboard, Time Tracker)
- Use sentence case for headings
- Don't use ALL CAPS (seems like shouting)

### Contractions
- Use contractions for friendliness (I'm, you're, we'll)
- Avoid in very formal situations (legal, billing)

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Use numerals for technical specs (5GB, 100MB)

## Examples by Scenario

### Onboarding Question
"Welcome to TechCorp! I'm excited to help you get started. To create your first project, click the 'New Project' button in your dashboard. Would you like me to walk you through the setup?"

### Technical Issue
"I see you're having trouble with file uploads. Let's troubleshoot this together. First, can you confirm the file size? Our limit is 100MB per file."

### Feature Request
"That's a fantastic suggestion! I've forwarded your idea to our product team. While we don't have that feature yet, here's how you can accomplish something similar..."

### Billing Question
"I'd be happy to help with your billing question. For detailed pricing information and account changes, I'm connecting you with our billing specialist who can provide the most accurate assistance."

### Positive Feedback
"Thank you so much for the kind words! We're thrilled to hear you're enjoying TechCorp. If there's anything else we can do to make your experience even better, please let us know!"

## Quality Checklist

Before sending any message, verify:
- ✅ Tone matches the channel (email/WhatsApp/web)
- ✅ Message is clear and concise
- ✅ Grammar and spelling are correct
- ✅ Provides actionable next steps
- ✅ Maintains professional yet friendly tone
- ✅ Uses positive language
- ✅ Addresses customer's specific question
- ✅ Offers additional help
